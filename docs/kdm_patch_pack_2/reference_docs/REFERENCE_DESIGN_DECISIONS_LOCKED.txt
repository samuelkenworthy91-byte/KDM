LOCKED DESIGN DECISIONS

1. Campaign principles are not normal innovations.
They are permanent settlement-wide choices triggered by story moments.

Principle triggers:
- First survivor death -> Death Principle
- First newborn / first child -> New Life Principle
- Lantern Year 5 -> Society Principle

2. Death Principle:
Graves:
- Every survivor death gives +1 Memory.
- Applies to the first death that triggered the choice.
- Applies automatically to all future deaths.

Cannibalism:
- Every survivor death gives +1 random basic resource.
- Applies to the first death that triggered the choice.
- Applies automatically to all future deaths.

3. New Life Principle:
Protect the Young:
- +10 percentage points intimacy success chance.
- -10 percentage points intimacy tragedy chance.
- All newborns gain +5 max HP.
- Applies to the newborn that triggered the choice.

Survival of the Fittest:
- +20 percentage points intimacy tragedy chance.
- All newborns gain a permanent personal +2 damage effect.
- All newborns gain +1 max Survival.
- Applies to the newborn that triggered the choice.

4. Society Principle:
Work Together:
- Once per Lantern Year, any one thing that costs exactly 1 Memory costs 0 instead.
- It can apply to innovation attempts, Rite of Forgetting, Memory Training, Weapon Drills, Quiet Night, or any future 1-Memory action.
- UI must show original cost, Work Together discount, final cost, and whether it is already used this Lantern Year.

Embrace the Dark:
- Affects rest stops only.
- Negative rest outcomes -10 percentage points.
- Neutral rest outcomes -10 percentage points.
- Positive rest outcomes +20 percentage points.
- Rest stop odds must still total 100%.

5. Hunt events:
- Most hunt events automatically choose the event survivor.
- Some special events may let the player choose a survivor.
- Events are roll-driven, modified by survivor/party/passives, not mostly choice-driven.

6. Nemesis encounters:
- One champion fights one-on-one.
- Nemesis fights should be far more deadly.
- Victory gives:
  - a few basic resources
  - possibly rare resources from a random unlocked monster/quarry
  - one very powerful nemesis champion card for the survivor who personally won
- Champion cards go into that survivor's deck.
- Champion cards should usually be Exhaust or otherwise limited because they are extremely powerful.

7. Fighting arts and proficiency:
- Fighting arts no longer add normal cards to the deck.
- Weapon proficiency no longer adds normal cards to the deck.
- They become passives.
- Some fighting arts may become active buttons on/near the survival action bar.
- Weapon proficiency uses all passives up to the current level.

8. Hunter panel flip:
- Each hunter panel should be flippable.
- Front shows current combat stats.
- Back shows active passives affecting that hunter:
  - fighting arts
  - active fighting art buttons
  - weapon proficiency
  - traits
  - scars
  - disorders
  - principles
  - gear passives
  - temporary hunt/combat modifiers

9. Signature weapons:
- Signature weapons should be visible as locked long-term targets.
- Their progress to unlock/milestones should be obvious.
- Normal gear unlocks from buildings.
- Signature weapons need signature unlock/progress, not just building unlock.
